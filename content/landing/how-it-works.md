## Step 1: Drop Your Content
### *Paste. Upload. Speak. Whatever works.*

**What you do:**
Open XueBaOS. Paste your lecture notes. Upload a PDF of your textbook chapter. Drop in your syllabus. Heck, speak your revision notes into your phone while you're on the MTR. XueBaOS parses everything.

**What happens behind the scenes:**
The AI ingestion engine reads your content and performs:

- **Entity extraction** — identifies key concepts, dates, formulas, definitions, and relationships
- **Hierarchy mapping** — organizes information into a knowledge tree (topic → subtopic → fact)
- **Difficulty tagging** — flags high-complexity items that need stronger mnemonics
- **Cross-reference detection** — spots connections between concepts across different sections
- **Gap analysis** — identifies what your notes might be missing based on syllabus requirements

You'll see a structured outline of everything the AI extracted — review it, adjust it, approve it.

> *This step takes roughly 30 seconds for a 20-page document.*

---

## Step 2: AI Builds Your World
### *Watch your notes transform into a spatial memory.*

**What happens:**
This is where the magic happens. XueBaOS's AI simultaneously:

1. **Designs your palace** — Assigns topics to rooms, subtopics to furniture, facts to objects. A biology unit on the circulatory system might become a grand chamber with the heart as a central throne, arteries as red-carpeted corridors, veins as blue return paths.

2. **Generates symbols** — Every abstract concept gets a concrete visual representation. "Osmosis" becomes a crowd of water molecules pushing through a semi-permeable bouncer at a club. "The Treaty of Versailles" becomes a broken scale dripping gold coins.

3. **Writes mnemonics** — Every fact gets a memorable story, acronym, or association. The weirder, the better. Your brain will *want* to remember these.

4. **Seeds your FSRS schedule** — Initial review intervals are set based on content complexity and your exam timeline.

5. **Populates your question bank** — Practice questions at every cognitive level are pre-generated and waiting.

**What you see:**
A fully visualized memory palace — rooms you can walk through, objects you can interact with. On desktop: a 3D environment. On mobile: a streamlined 2D map. Every object is clickable, revealing its associated fact, symbol, and mnemonic.

> *This step takes 1-3 minutes depending on content volume. Grab a coffee.*

---

## Step 3: Walk. Recall. Master.
### *This is where you become a 學霸.*

**What you do:**

**Walk your palace daily.** Navigate through your rooms. Click objects to test recall. XueBaOS tracks which facts you remember and which you don't.

**Follow your FSRS prompts.** When the algorithm says you're about to forget something — and only then — XueBaOS prompts you to review it. No wasted reviews. No forgotten facts.

**Answer deep questions.** Move from "I recognize this" to "I can explain this." The question bank pushes you up Bloom's taxonomy, one level at a time.

**Watch your analytics.** Your dashboard shows you exactly what you know, what's fading, and what you've mastered. This is data-driven revision, the way a quant trader approaches the market.

**The loop:** Walk → Recall → Get tested → Identify gaps → Re-encode → Walk again.

Each cycle strengthens the neural pathways. Each walk through your palace makes the memory more durable, more accessible, more automatic. By exam day, recalling a fact feels less like "trying to remember" and more like "walking into the right room and picking it up."

> *Most students achieve 90%+ retention on palace content within 3-4 walkthrough cycles.*
